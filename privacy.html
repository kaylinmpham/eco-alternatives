Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 8, 2026

Mint Condition is a Chrome browser extension that displays sustainability ratings for fashion brands and surfaces secondhand alternatives from eBay while you shop. This policy explains what information the extension accesses and how it is used.

Data we do not collect

Mint Condition does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personally identifiable information. The extension does not track browsing history, record which pages you visit, or build any profile of user behavior.

What the extension reads

When you open the Mint Condition side panel on a product page, the extension reads product metadata from that page — specifically the product name, brand, and image URL. This is done locally in your browser using standard page markup (JSON-LD and Open Graph tags).

This product information is sent to a backend service operated by the developer solely to fetch a sustainability score and search for matching secondhand listings on eBay. The information is used only to fulfill this request and is not stored or logged.

Third-party services

eBay: Product image and search queries are sent to the eBay Browse API to retrieve secondhand listings. eBay's own privacy policy governs their handling of these requests.

Google Fonts: The extension's side panel loads fonts from Google Fonts CDN. Google may log these requests per their standard CDN policies.

Cloudflare: The developer's backend is hosted on Cloudflare Workers. Cloudflare processes requests in accordance with their privacy policy and may log standard request metadata (IP address, timestamp) as part of their infrastructure.

Data storage

The extension uses Chrome's local storage (chrome.storage) to temporarily cache results for the current browsing session. This data is stored locally on your device and is not transmitted anywhere.

Permissions

The extension requests access to all URLs in order to detect product information across any fashion retail website. This permission is used only to read product metadata on the active tab when you open the side panel. The extension does not access tabs you are not actively viewing.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the extension evolves. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, contact the developer through the Chrome Web Store listing.
